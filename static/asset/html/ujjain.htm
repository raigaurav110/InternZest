History



Ujjain has a long political history. Protohistoric and early Iron Age material has been found in abundance in the excavations from the Citadel area of Ujjain. According to the Puranas and the Mahabharata, Vrishni-Veer Krishna, and Balarama came here to study at Guru Sandipani's ashram. Mitravrinda, Krishna's wife, was a Ujjain princess. Vind and Anuvind, his two brothers, had achieved heroic speed while fighting on the side of the Kauravas during the Mahabharata battle. In the sixth century AD, Ujjain was ruled by a magnificent ruler named Chand Pradyota. Other Indian rulers used to dread him. Duhita Vasavadatta and Vatsanresh Udayan's love tale is well-known in history; following the Pradyot dynasty, Ujjain became a member of the Magadha Empire.
Emperor Vikramaditya ruled Ujjain; his father was King Gandharvasena; the Shakas were named after a monk named Kalakacharya; the Shakas defeated the ruler of Ujjain, Gandharvasena. They grabbed possession of Ujjain, but the Shakas were defeated by Vikramaditya, the son of King Gandharvasena. Vikramaditya vanquished the Shakas and re-established his predecessors' dominion in Ujjain, earning him the title "Great Emperor of this Country."
Evidence of Ujjayini's historicity was discovered 600 years ago. Avanti district was also one of India's sixteen districts at the time. Avanti was divided into two halves, North and South, with Ujjain as the northern capital and Mahishmati as the southern capital. At the time, the emperor on the throne was Chandrapradyota. Pradyota's family ruled Ujjain until the third century.

















Magadha


According to Jain texts, King Khadirsar ruled in Magadha; his capital was Ujjain; the time of his rule was 386 BCE; King Khadirsar's father's name was Kunika, who was the king of Magadha during 414 BCE; King Khadirsar's wife's name was Chelama; initially King Khadirsar was a follower of Buddhism, but influenced by Queen Chelama's teachings.






























Discover



Mahakaleshwar Temple,Ujjain
Mahakaleshwar Temple is an important religious site in Ujjain. This is one of the most incredible spots to see in Ujjain. Lord Shiva is honoured in this temple. One of Lord Shiva's 12 Jyotirlingas can be found here. This temple is well-known in Madhya Pradesh, and it is said to be well-known throughout India. The Shivling of Mahakaleshwar is said to be a self-styled Shivling. It came from the soil itself. It is yet to be installed. Devotees travel long distances to see Lord Shiva here.It feels amazing to be here, and the Mahakaleshwar temple is famous for Lord Shiva's Bhasma Aarti. The pyre ashes are used to perform Lord Shiva's ashes. As a result, many people attend this aarti and perform Lord Shiva's aarti. If you come to Ujjain, you must visit the Mahakaleshwar temple. There are numerous amenities available here. The Mahakaleshwar temple provides housing and dining options. With permission from the temple official, you may stay here.

Nagchandreshwar Temple,Ujjain
The Nagchandreshwar shrine is a notable shrine located within the Mahakaleshwar Temple in Ujjain. This temple is devoted to Nag Devta and is only open on the occasion of Nag Panchami. This temple only opens once a year. That is why there is such a large crowd in this temple. A lovely idol of Lord Shiva can be found at this temple. In which Lord Shiva is seated on Vishnu's Sheshnag rather than Mount Kailash. Many individuals come here to visit this statue and leave satisfied. The Mahakaleshwar temple's third floor houses this temple.

Shri Khamba Mata Mandir,Ujjain
Shri Beis Khamba Mata Mandir is a significant religious site in Ujjain. This temple honours Mahamaya and Mahalaya Mata. These are two sisters. You've arrived to this temple. Both of these deities are available for viewing. It is said that this temple has 24 pillars. That is why this temple is known as the 24 Khamba Temple. This temple is located in the centre of Ujjain. You are welcome to come and explore.

Shri Kaal Bhairav Temple,Ujjain
Shri Kaal Bhairav Temple is a significant religious site in Ujjain. This is the best attraction in Ujjain. Shri Kaal Bhairav ji is honoured in this temple. The temple contains a lovely statue of Kaal Bhairav ji. Aside from that, it is stated that Shri Kaal Bhairav ji eats the prasad of liquor in this temple. Around the temple, there are numerous liquor stores where alcohol is served as prasad. This temple is quite old, and many people have faith in it. This temple is located on the banks of the Shipra River in the city of Ujjain. So you are welcome to explore this temple.
